My journey towards creating a new tech community on social media

Tech Communnity, Oct 16, 2021

After spending most of 2017, 2018 and 2019 participating in at least one social media tech group on Facebook, Twitch and YouTube, I decided to create my own Facebook Group called The Art of Tech: Not Playing with a Full Tech back in March 2020 after running some polls and talking with a few of my friends and aquaintence about creating an online community. We started out on Facebook groups. I created this group because there are so many social media tech groups and creators out there who produce some of the worst drama, poor quality content and not only that inacurate information, not only that there is a large tech gap and disparity between those with.

At the time of this writing we have 200 members on Facebook and 40+ members in our Discord.

The purpose goal of this group is to have a safe place to discuss and share our tech lives, the many, many tech things we do that help us in our lives as well as others. We are all Windows, Unix and Linux users of various levels who like to share our expertise with others no matter how basic or advanced they are.

This group is for members who are interested in Tech and IT and especially related advice. This group is designed to get you involved in sharing your experiences with the Windows, Linux and Unix operating systems and the apps that run in Linux and Unix or one of its derivatives, and technology in general. If new to Linux or Unix, the group can help. This site has been expanded to include all technology-related concepts, not just Linux- and Unix-related technology based. We hope to expand our membership base in the US. Like most new groups we have had some growing pains. Most of our initial members were viewers and subscribers of a YouTube channel.

Most of us are professional and hobbyist level tech enthusiasists who like being around other like minded people.
